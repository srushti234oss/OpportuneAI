Fellowships are the hidden gems — longer, more impactful, and less competitive than internships. They come with mentorship, stipend, and often change career trajectories.

💡 What's the difference between an internship and a fellowship?

Fellowships are typically 3–12 months (longer than internships), often focus on research, social impact, or leadership development, and carry more prestige. They usually involve a stipend, accommodation, and a structured mentorship program. Think of them as "internships with a mission."
